# Case Study: Functional, UI & ADA Testing for Interactive Video E-Commerce Platform (NDA)

## Overview
- **Client type:** Interactive storytelling / digital ads platform
- **Industry:** Marketing & Branding / E-Commerce Video Content (USA)
- **Service provided:** Manual QA — functional testing, UI testing, ADA/accessibility testing for interactive video content
- **Tags:** Manual Testing, Functional Testing, UI Testing, ADA Testing, Accessibility Testing, Interactive Video QA, Asana, Web App Testing, UX Feedback

---

## The Challenge
A US-based interactive video platform producing cinematic, shoppable brand content for major retailers needed QA coverage across a large volume of interactive video releases. The platform's core product — interactive videos where users shape brand stories in real time — required both standard functional/UI validation and strict ADA compliance testing before going live with a multinational retail client.

Key pain points:
- 200+ interactive videos requiring individual QA inspection, delivered in irregular batches (5–20 per week)
- ADA compliance requirements demanded structured accessibility testing with agreed tooling and checklists — not a standard QA workflow
- No existing QA process for the video content format — testing approach had to be defined from scratch
- Scope expanded beyond initial estimate, requiring a shift from fixed-scope to part-time ongoing engagement
- Bug reporting had to follow the client's own Asana workflow to maintain consistency across teams

---

## What I Did
- Studied the platform's interactive video product in depth to determine what required testing and how to execute it effectively within the client's delivery model
- Prepared a **functional and UI testing checklist** covering interactive video behavior, button logic, branching flows, and visual consistency
- Prepared a dedicated **ADA compliance checklist** — agreed tooling and test scope with the client's team before execution, covering subtitle presence, keyboard navigation, contrast, and assistive technology compatibility
- Received and processed video bundles on a rolling basis, assigning and executing QA inspection across each set as delivered
- Logged **all defects in Asana** following the client's established defect reporting flow — maintaining consistent reporting format across the engagement
- Provided **UX improvement recommendations** alongside bug reports — covering video quality, button logic, and interaction design — which the client's team implemented
- Transitioned engagement model mid-project from fixed estimate to **part-time ongoing** as video volume exceeded initial scope

---

## Results
- **5–15 bugs detected per video bundle** — all logged and actioned by the client's team before go-live
- **Missing subtitles** identified as a recurring ADA compliance issue — resolved across the video library before release to the multinational retail client
- Videos released post-QA were measurably more functional, accessible, and user-friendly compared to their pre-testing versions
- UX recommendations implemented by the client team, improving interaction logic and overall video experience
- Flexible engagement model sustained quality coverage despite irregular delivery schedules and expanding scope

---

## What Made the Difference
Treating ADA compliance as a first-class deliverable — not an afterthought — required agreeing on tooling and scope upfront rather than applying generic accessibility checks. That alignment with the client team meant every accessibility finding was immediately actionable, and the subtitle issue that recurred across bundles was caught early enough to fix systematically rather than one video at a time.
