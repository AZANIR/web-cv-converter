# Case Study: Functional & UI QA for AI-Powered Sales Assistant in EU Cosmetics Marketplace (NDA)

## Overview
- **Client type:** B2C e-commerce marketplace
- **Industry:** Professional Cosmetics / Beauty Retail (EU)
- **Service provided:** Manual QA — functional testing, UI testing across web and mobile
- **Tags:** Manual Testing, Functional Testing, UI Testing, AI Assistant QA, Chat Testing, Voice Assistant Testing, CRM Integration Testing, Mobile Testing, Web Testing, E-commerce QA

---

## The Challenge
A leading EU distributor of professional cosmetic products (5,000+ SKUs) was rolling out a custom AI-powered assistant across its website, mobile app, and call center — replacing a manual support team across three phases. The assistant handled product search, recommendations, add-to-cart, voice interactions, and CRM-integrated upsell/cross-sell logic.

Testing this system required validating not just a UI, but a complex, multi-phase AI-driven product with natural language flows, voice interfaces, and live business system integrations.

Key pain points:
- AI-generated responses needed validation for accuracy, relevance, and domain correctness across 5,000+ cosmetics products
- Three distinct phases of rollout — chat assistant, voice interface, intelligent selling — each requiring separate QA cycles
- Cross-platform coverage required: web storefront, mobile app (beauty professionals and salons), and voice call flows
- CRM, order tracking, and cart recovery integrations introduced complex data flow dependencies
- UI/UX consistency needed across chat, voice, and mobile touchpoints throughout a 6-month delivery

---

## What I Did
- Conducted **functional testing** across all three rollout phases:
  - **Phase 1 (Chat Assistant):** Validated product search accuracy, FAQ response correctness, recommendation relevance, and add-to-cart functionality on web and mobile
  - **Phase 2 (Voice Assistant):** Tested voice recognition flows, natural dialog handling, product inquiry responses, delivery/policy information accuracy, and live agent handoff logic
  - **Phase 3 (Intelligent Selling):** Validated upsell/cross-sell suggestion logic, proactive cart recovery triggers, CRM personalization accuracy, real-time order status responses, and interaction logging
- Performed **UI testing** across web and mobile app surfaces — verifying chat widget behavior, voice interface components, and responsive layout consistency
- Validated **CRM and business system integrations** — ensuring order data, customer history, and assistant interactions were logged and retrieved correctly
- Executed **regression testing** across phases to ensure new features did not break previously validated assistant behavior
- Covered **edge cases and negative flows** in conversational logic — ambiguous inputs, out-of-scope queries, failed API responses, and escalation to live agents

---

## Results
- Functional and UI QA delivered across all three phases within a 6-month project timeline
- AI assistant launched stably across web and mobile — contributing to an **18.8% total sales increase** by end of project
- **6.7% conversion uplift** supported by validated fast, expert-level assistant responses
- **6% AOV increase** from contextual recommendation flows tested and confirmed accurate pre-launch
- **5% order volume growth** supported by reliable cart recovery and retention flows
- Call center reduced from 11 employees to 2 supervisors — AI-handled first-line support validated as production-ready
- Full ROI achieved in under six months

---

## What Made the Difference
Testing an AI assistant requires a different mindset than testing deterministic software — responses vary, flows branch unpredictably, and accuracy is domain-specific. Focusing QA effort on the business-critical paths (recommendations, cart recovery, CRM sync) while validating edge cases in conversational logic gave the team confidence that the assistant would behave reliably in production, not just in demos.
