# Case Study: Manual QA for Kids' Video Communication & Content Discovery iOS App (NDA)

## Overview
- **Client type:** Consumer mobile app startup
- **Industry:** Social Media / Kids & Family Technology (Roblox / Video Platform)
- **Service provided:** Manual QA Engineer — functional testing, usability testing, regression testing, bug tracking
- **Tags:** Manual Testing, Functional Testing, Usability Testing, Mobile App Testing, iOS Testing, Regression Testing, Bug Reporting, Exploratory Testing, Voice Feature Testing, Kids App QA

---

## The Challenge
A video communication and content discovery iOS app — designed for children and internet beginners of all ages — needed thorough manual QA to validate its core promises: ease of use for young users, accurate friend status display, personalized content recommendations, and accessible voice search functionality. The app had to work reliably for users who could not type or spell, making both functional accuracy and usability critical.

Key pain points:
- Target audience included children and tech beginners — usability and accessibility needed to meet a higher bar than typical consumer apps
- Multiple interconnected features to validate: video calling, friend status, content recommendation, voice search, and narrated results
- Integration with external video platforms added complexity to search and content validation
- No room for regression — bugs in friend status, content curation, or voice commands would directly degrade the experience for vulnerable users

---

## What I Did
- Executed full-cycle **manual testing** across all key app features, mimicking real user interactions from the perspective of children and internet beginners
- Conducted **usability testing** to verify the app was intuitive across all ages — confirming that friend online statuses were clearly visible and that manual availability checks via messaging were genuinely eliminated
- Validated the **content recommendation system** — verifying that topic tiles were easy to navigate, recommended videos matched user interests, and beginner-friendly content was correctly curated by default
- Tested **search functionality** for accuracy, speed, and relevance — including advanced search integrations with external video platforms
- Evaluated **voice search** for ease of use and command accuracy — confirming it worked reliably for users who could not type or spell
- Verified **narration of search results** in video format — assessing clarity and comprehensibility for the target audience
- Performed **regression testing** throughout the engagement to ensure new changes did not break previously validated functionality
- Documented all findings in structured **bug reports** with step-by-step reproduction instructions, expected vs. actual results, and improvement recommendations
- Delivered a full set of test plan, test cases, and test execution reports with pass/fail outcomes

---

## Results
- Engagement lasted **over one year** — consistently identifying important bugs across feature releases
- All five testing areas fully covered: usability, content recommendation, search, voice search, and narration
- Bug reports enabled the development team to resolve issues before they reached child users
- Client awarded a **5.0 rating** with public feedback: *"Hard working, communicative, and found lots of important bugs for our team. I highly recommend him for people who want to work with highly motivated and talented people."*
- App delivered an improved, more reliable experience for its target audience of children and internet beginners

---

## What Made the Difference
Testing from the user's perspective — not just checking functionality in isolation — was key for a kids' platform. Validating that voice search worked for users who couldn't type, that friend statuses were genuinely easy to read at a glance, and that content curation actually felt beginner-friendly required a user-centric mindset throughout, not just a checklist.
