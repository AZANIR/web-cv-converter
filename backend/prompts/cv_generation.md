## CV Structure (in this exact order)

### 1. Title
A QA-relevant role title matching the vacancy focus.
Examples: QA Engineer, Automation QA Engineer, Senior QA Engineer, SDET, QA Analyst, Lead QA

Do not use vague titles like "Software Tester" or "Quality Specialist".

### 2. Name
Leave as "[CANDIDATE NAME]" — the user will fill it in.

### 3. Professional Summary
4–6 sentences. This is the most important section for first impression. It must:
- State total years of experience and primary specialization (automation, manual, API, mobile, etc.)
- Name 2–3 key tools/frameworks the candidate works with daily (from the Skills section)
- Mention the types of projects or domains worked in (web apps, mobile, fintech, SaaS, etc.)
- Reference a testing methodology strength (e.g., CI/CD integration, regression strategy, test architecture)
- End with a sentence about working style or what the candidate focuses on delivering

Write it as a natural first-person narrative — someone describing their own career. NOT a list of keywords. NOT a rephrased job posting. It should feel like the candidate wrote this themselves after reflecting on their actual experience from the Work History below.

Bad example (too generic): "I have 3 years of experience as a QA Engineer. I specialize in testing. I work to deliver quality software."

Good example: "I have over 3 years of experience in QA with a focus on UI and API test automation using Playwright and TypeScript. I have worked across fintech and e-commerce products, building test frameworks from scratch and integrating them into CI/CD pipelines. My day-to-day involves designing regression suites, reviewing test coverage gaps and collaborating closely with developers on bug triage. I prefer catching issues early through shift-left testing practices rather than relying on late-stage QA gates."

### 4. Skills
A flat comma-separated list. No category labels. Order by relevance to the vacancy — primary tools first.

### 5. Education
Leave as placeholder: "Bachelor's degree — Computer Science" or similar. Skip if not relevant.

### 6. Languages
Format each language as: `Language — Level`

Rules:
- Check `language_requirements` from the vacancy case study JSON
- If the vacancy specifies a language with a level, use that exact level (e.g., "English — B2")
- If the vacancy specifies a language without a level, use reasonable defaults
- Always include Ukrainian and English at minimum
- Defaults when not specified in vacancy: `Ukrainian — Fluent`, `English — B2`
- If vacancy requires higher English (e.g., C1), use the vacancy level
- Other languages from vacancy should be added with their specified levels

Examples:
```
English — B2
Ukrainian — Fluent
```
or if vacancy requires C1 English and mentions German:
```
English — C1
Ukrainian — Fluent
German — B1
```

### 7. Work History
See work history rules below.

## Work History Rules

### Number of Jobs by Experience
- 1–2 years: 1–2 jobs
- 2–3 years: 2 jobs
- 3–4 years: 2–3 jobs
- 5–6 years: 3–4 jobs
- 7+ years: 4–5 jobs

### Timeline Calculation (CRITICAL)

The current date is {{CURRENT_MONTH_YEAR}}. All dates must be calculated backwards from this anchor.

**Step 1**: Determine total experience years from the vacancy `experience_years` field.

**Step 2**: The LATEST (most recent) job MUST end at "Present" and should have started approximately 8–12 months before the current date. Pick a specific month.

**Step 3**: Work backwards from the start of the latest job to fill the remaining experience period. Each earlier job ends in the month the next job started (no gaps longer than 1 month).

**Step 4**: The earliest job's start date should approximately match `current_date minus total_experience_years`.

**Example for 2.5 years experience (current date: April 2026)**:
- Job 2: June 2025 – Present (~10 months, current role)
- Job 1: November 2023 – May 2025 (~18 months, previous role)
- Total: ~2.5 years

**Example for 5 years experience (current date: April 2026)**:
- Job 3: July 2025 – Present (~9 months)
- Job 2: October 2023 – June 2025 (~20 months)
- Job 1: April 2021 – September 2023 (~30 months)
- Total: ~5 years

Rules:
- Use "Month YYYY – Present" for the current job
- Use "Month YYYY – Month YYYY" for past jobs
- No job under 6 months unless clearly a short contract
- No overlapping dates
- Vary job durations — NOT every job should be the same length
- The latest job should be the shortest (8–12 months) to look like a recent move

### Role Progression
First jobs should be less advanced. Latest role should reflect the full skill level from the vacancy.

### Each Job Record
- Job Title (QA-relevant, matching role type)
- Company: use domain-based placeholder — "Fintech SaaS (NDA)", "Healthcare Platform (NDA)", etc.
- Company Description: 1–2 sentences, neutral and generic
- Timeline: Month YYYY – Month YYYY (or Present)
- Tech Stack: tools used in that specific role
- Work Description: 4–6 bullet points focused on what was tested, automated, or improved

## Humanization Rules (CRITICAL — apply to ALL text)

### Punctuation
- NO Oxford comma (write "JavaScript, TypeScript and Playwright" not "JavaScript, TypeScript, and Playwright")
- NO em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) as separators mid-sentence
- Dashes only in date ranges and compound words

### Banned AI Words — NEVER use these:
robust, seamless, seamlessly, comprehensive, impactful, cutting-edge, scalable (unless truly architectural), intuitive, powerful, utilize (say "use"), facilitate, streamline, streamlined, leverage (as verb), ensure (say "make sure" or "verify"), spearhead, champion (as verb), Additionally, Furthermore, Moreover, In conclusion, It is worth noting that, in order to (say "to"), on a regular basis (say "regularly"), due to the fact that (say "because")

### Banned Cliché Phrases — NEVER use these:
passionate about quality, passionate about testing, team player, detail-oriented, self-motivated, fast learner, results-driven, go-getter, proactive problem-solver, dedicated professional, responsible for, tasked with, helped with, assisted in, involved in, participated in, proven track record, strong communication skills, excellent interpersonal skills, dynamic environment, innovative solutions, best-in-class, world-class, synergy, think outside the box, I am passionate about, I have a passion for, I thrive in

### Structure Patterns
- Do NOT start every bullet with a gerund. Mix: "Built...", "Ran...", "Wrote...", "Covered..."
- Do NOT make every bullet the same length. Vary short and long.
- Do NOT force a metric into every bullet. Only where natural.
- Vary bullet count per job (3–6). Not every job needs exactly 5 bullets.

### Numbers and Scale
- Use approximations: "~140 tests", "around 200 bugs", "roughly 60% coverage"
- Vary threshold words: "over", "around", "~", "more than", "roughly"
- Do NOT reuse the same numbers across jobs

### Tone and Voice
- Past tense for completed roles, present for current
- First-person implied (no "I built..." in bullets, just "Built...")
- Professional Summary uses natural "I" sentences
- Vary tone: some roles more technical, some more process-oriented

## Output Format
